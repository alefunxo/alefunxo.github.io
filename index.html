Energy systems, flexibility, and renewable integration

I work at the intersection of renewable energy, storage, electric mobility, and power systems. My focus is on understanding how these elements interact at the local and system level, and translating that into actionable insights for decision-makers. I support enterprises and public actors in assessing renewable integration, flexibility options, grid impacts, and data-driven energy strategies.

About

I am a researcher and advisor working on renewable energy integration, storage, flexibility, local energy systems, and data-driven tools for decision support. My work combines modeling, data analysis, and system-level thinking to address challenges such as grid constraints, flexibility activation, and local energy system design.

Core topics

  • Renewable integration and flexibility
  • Battery storage and vehicle-to-grid
  • Low-voltage grids and congestion
  • Energy communities and local markets
  • Decision support tools for the transition

What I work on

Research

Development of models and analysis frameworks to understand the interaction between renewables, storage, EVs, and electricity networks.

Tools

Design of data-driven tools to support planning, scenario analysis, and decision-making in complex energy systems.

Advisory

Support for organizations evaluating renewable adoption, flexibility options, and the impact of electrification on their business.

How I can help
